    Age, Biography and Wiki

    Gerald Ford was born Leslie Lynch King Jr. on July 14, 1913 in Omaha, Nebraska. He was the 38th President of the United States, serving from 1974 to 1977. He was the only president to have never been elected to the office of the President or Vice President. Ford attended the University of Michigan, where he earned a degree in economics and political science. After college, he served in the U.S. Navy during World War II. After the war, he returned to Michigan and attended Yale Law School, graduating in 1941. Ford began his political career in 1948, when he was elected to the U.S. House of Representatives. He served in the House for 25 years, becoming the House Minority Leader in 1965. In 1973, he was appointed Vice President of the United States by President Richard Nixon. After Nixon resigned in 1974, Ford became the 38th President of the United States. During his presidency, Ford pardoned Nixon for any crimes he may have committed during the Watergate scandal. He also signed the Helsinki Accords, which improved relations between the United States and the Soviet Union. Ford died on December 26, 2006 at the age of 93. He was buried in his hometown of Grand Rapids, Michigan.
