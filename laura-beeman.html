    Laura Beeman is one of the most popular and richest Basketball Coach who was born on April 16, 1968 in San Bernardino, United States. Laura Lynne Beeman (born April 16 1968) is an American college basketball coach. She is the head coach of women’s basketball for the University of Hawaii.

    On the junior-college at the junior college level, Beeman became a women’s basketball assistant coach at Mt. San Antonio College in 1994. Beeman also attended Azusa Pacific University the following year, and graduated with an advanced degrees in education for physical therapy in the year 1996. Beeman was also elevated to the position of head coach for Mt. San Antonio in 1995. In 1995 at Mt. San Antonio, Beeman led the team towards the winning of four CCCAA titles (2004 2006, 2007 and 2007, 2008) and 10 South Coast Conference titles, and a 390-110-record during 15 seasons from 1995 through the year 2010.

    In 2012, the University of Hawaii at Manoa hired Beeman as head coach for Hawaii Rainbow Wahine basketball. In four seasons, Beeman led Hawaii to three WNIT appearances (2013, WNIT, and 2015) and one NCAA Tournament appearance (2016). Hawaii won the Big West Conference regular season title in 2015 and Big West Tournament in 2016.

    In addition, as graduate students, Beeman became a women’s basketbal assistant coach at the University of Redlands in 1992. She finished her M.Ed. in counseling in 1994.
