    Ryan Getzlaf (born May 10, 1985) is a Canadian professional ice hockey centre who plays for the Anaheim Ducks of the National Hockey League. He is currently serving as the team’s captain (NHL). Getzlaf was selected in the first round, 19th overall, in the 2003 NHL Entry Draft by the Anaheim Ducks. He has spent his entire professional career with the team, and he is one of the franchise’s all-time top scorers. He has represented his country in two NHL All-Star Games and was a part of the Ducks’ Stanley Cup-winning squad from 2007. Taken together, Getzlaf’s contributions as a playmaker and power forward have resulted in seven straight seasons as the Ducks’ top assist producer, including a franchise-high 66 in 2008–09. 

    While still a junior, Getzlaf spent four seasons with the Calgary Hitmen, earning two all-star selections in the Western Hockey League (WHL). On a number of occasions, he has represented Canada in international competitions. Getzlaf was a part of the Canadian World Junior Championship team in 2005, which was widely regarded as the best in the country’s history. He also competed in and earned a silver medal in the 2008 IIHF World Championship. Getzlaf was a member of the Canadian teams that won gold medals at the 2010 and 2014 Winter Olympics. He is a two-time Olympian.
