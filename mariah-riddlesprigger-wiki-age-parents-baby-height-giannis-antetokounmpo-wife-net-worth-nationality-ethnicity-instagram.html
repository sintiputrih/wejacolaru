    Mariah Riddlesprigger Biography – Mariah Riddlesprigger Wiki

    Mariah Riddlesprigger (full name Mariah Danae Riddlesprigger) is Giannis Antetokounmpo’s girlfriend and a former volleyball player. She graduated from Bullard High School in 2010 and from Rice University in 2014, with a degree in sports management and sociology, with a minor in business, according to her LinkedIn page.

    She was a member of the Student-Athlete Advisory Committee. From March 2011 to 2013, she worked at Rice University Ticket Office. From May 2015 to June 2016, she worked as a basketball operations intern for the Philadelphia 76ers.

    She is the creator of the clothing line, Sincerely Mariah. Mariah “created this brand to remind each and every one of you how exceptionally unique you are! My goal is to provide a product that discourages self doubt and shouts that you are worthy! Inspired by all of the remarkable people I have met throughout the years. I hope that this brand serves as a constant reminder to embrace who you are, unapologetically!,” according to a statement on the Sincerely Mariah website.

    Mariah Riddlesprigger Volleyball

    Mariah played collegiate volleyball for Rio University from 2010 to 2013. She played in 29 matches, making 23 starts as senior. She was named the C-USA Defensive Player of the Week (Nov. 18, 2013). She recorded a season-high 16 kills vs. LSU (Sept. 7) and posted double-digit kills in five matches. She compiled two double-doubles, recorded a season-high 18 digs vs. UAB (Nov. 15), hit a season-best .706 (12 kills, 17 attempts) in her home finale vs. Louisiana Tech (Nov. 17), had nine service aces, was fourth on team with 204 digs, had 26 total blocks, and was sixth on team with 141 kills.

    A 2010 graduate of Bullard High School, Mariah played for coach Kieran Roblee. She was team captain and team MVP as senior. In 2009, she posted 342 kills, 212 digs, 22 aces, 17 blocks and a .433 kill percentage. She led the Knights to the Metro Athletic Conference title and was named league MVP award.

    As a junior, was a first team all-district selection and second team all-conference and the Knights’ team MVP and team captain. She also competed as a high jumper in track and field with best mark of 5-4 and 11.92 in 100m. She was active in the African American Student Union, Junior LARCs, Pay It Forward, Special Olympics, Northpointe Community Church and Kid’s Day.
