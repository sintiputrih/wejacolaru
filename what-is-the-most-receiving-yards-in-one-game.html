    In the world of American football, one of the most captivating and thrilling aspects of the game is the individual records that players strive to achieve.

    This record has been a source of awe and inspiration for football fans and players alike. 

    Understanding Receiving Yards

    Before delving into the record for the most receiving yards in a single game, it is essential to understand what receiving yards entail.

    In American football, receiving yards refer to the total yardage gained by a receiver as a result of catching passes during a game.

    This metric is a pivotal measure of a wide receiver's performance and is indicative of their ability to contribute to their team's offensive success.

    The Record-Breaking Achievement

    The record for the most receiving yards in a single game is a testament to the extraordinary talent and skill of the players who have achieved this feat

    . As of the last update in September 2021, the record is held by Flipper Anderson, a former wide receiver for the Los Angeles Rams.

    Anderson achieved this remarkable milestone on November 26, 1989, in a game against the New Orleans Saints. During this game, Anderson amassed an astonishing 336 receiving yards, setting a new standard for excellence in the NFL.
