    Who is Blake Shelton?

    Blake Shelton is an American country music singer and television personality. In 2001, he made his debut with the single “Austin” from his first album, Blake Shelton.

    Blake Shelton biography and career

    Blake Tollison Shelton, born in Ada, Oklahoma, to Dorothy Ann (née Bristol) and Richard Lee “Dick” Shelton, had a passion for singing from a young age. At just 12 years old, he learned to play the guitar with the guidance of his uncle. By 15, he had already written his first song, and at 16, he was honored with a Denbo Diamond Award in his home state. Tragically, on November 13, 1990, his older brother Richie Shelton lost his life in a car accident at the age of 24.

    After completing high school at 17, Shelton made the move to Nashville, Tennessee, in pursuit of a career in singing. He secured a job at a music publishing company and, in 1997, with the help of Bobby Braddock, he landed a production contract with Sony Music.

    Shelton’s albums, such as “The Dreamer” (2003) and “Blake Shelton’s Barn & Grill” (2004), achieved gold and platinum status respectively. His fourth album, “Pure BS” (2007), was re-released in 2008 with a cover of Michael Bublé’s popular song “Home” as a bonus track. He continued to release successful albums, including “Startin’ Fires” (2008), the extended plays “Hillbilly Bone” and “All About Tonight” in 2010, and the albums “Red River Blue” (2011), “Based on a True Story…” (2013), “Bringing Back the Sunshine” (2014), “If I’m Honest” (2016), “Texoma Shore” (2017), and “Fully Loaded: God’s Country” (2019).

    As of December 2020, Shelton has achieved an impressive 40 charted singles, with 28 of them reaching number one on the charts. Notably, his 11th number one single, “Doin’ What She Likes,” set a new record for the most consecutive number one singles in the history of the Country Airplay chart, spanning 24 years. Throughout his career, he has garnered nine Grammy Award nominations, including two for Best Country Album.

    In addition to his music career, Shelton has also made a name for himself as a judge on various televised singing competitions. He appeared as a judge on shows like Nashville Star and Clash of the Choirs, and served as a coach on NBC’s The Voice.
