    Ali Harper On Selling The OC

    The second season of Selling the OC is here. The Selling Sunset spinoff offers yet another serving of amazing homes and never-ending office drama following a popular initial run in August 2022.

    For the second season, everyone from the first one is back. The show’s star is The Oppenheim Group owner Jason Oppenheim, and despite the fact that Brett Oppenheim officially left the brokerage to open his own, he nevertheless makes an appearance in season two. The remaining cast members include Austin Victoria, Tyler Stanaland, Polly Brindle, Lauren Shortt, Kayla Cardona, Gio Helou, Brandi Marshall, Sean Palmieri, and Alexandra Hall, Alexandra Jarvis, and Alexandra Rose.

    The only newcomer this season, aside from the original cast members, is Ali (Alexandra) Harper. That does imply that there are now four Alexandras to monitor. Fortunately, the former Miss Tennessee and native of Nashville goes by Ali to provide some “variety.”

    A native of Nashville, Tennessee who joins the Orange County office of The Oppenheim Group as a prospective agent with interest in the housing market. She also previously competed in pageants, winning Miss Tennessee USA in 2018 and even finishing in the Top 10.

    How Much Is Ali Harper Net Worth?

    According to The Cinemaholic Ali Harper has amassed a whopping net worth of $750K.

    Per Ali’s LinkedIn began her career as a summer intern for News Channel 5 in Nashville, Tennessee, from May 2013 to August 2013. She then enrolled at the University of Tennessee-Knoxville in 2013 to pursue a degree in journalism, communication, and related fields, where she remained until 2015.

    Ali joined Sinclair Broadcast Group as an account executive in August 2015 and worked there through August 2016. She joined Carol Swain Enterprises in June 2016 as a Public Relations Communications Manager, a position she maintained until May 2019. She also worked as a content writer for Country Common from November 2016 to June 2017, performing four tasks every day.

    Ali joined the Miss Universe Organization in October 2017 in order to compete in the Miss Tennessee USA Pageant. “I competed and won the title of Miss Tennessee USA in 2018 and placed Top 10 in Miss USA, all the while I was juggling three jobs and driving for Uber Eats on the side,” Ali told People.

    Ali later cut ties with the Miss Universe Organization in October 2018. The reality TV personality started working for herself as a website designer and content creator in December 2017, but she quit in March 2020. In April 2019, Ali’s tenure with “Music City” likewise came to an end.

    Ali began working as a Mobile Specialist Consultant with Engage by Cell in March 2020 and remained there through March 2022. Following her experience in Nashville, “Selling the OC” provides a thorough account of her path to becoming a real estate agent in California. She began training to obtain her license in order to demonstrate to the world her abilities after receiving a standing offer to join the Oppenheim Group in Orange County, California.

    Who Are Ali Harper Parents?

    Ali referred to her Nashville family as the “Kardashians of the South” when she first met the Selling The OC team. Her parents are named Sharon and Edward Harper. According to Sharon’s Facebook, she and her husband married in March 1989.

    Hailing from Brentwood, Tennessee, Sharon is 66 years old meanwhile Edward is currently age 65.

    Edward is very close to his daughter and has been featured on several of her IG posts. As a proud dad, in September 2016, Ali posted, “So proud of my wonderful father. Today he received the Lifetime Achievement Award for the Gospel Music Association, and it’s his birthday! He has never put his business before his love for Jesus. Ed, you are the man, and I love you!

    Ed is currently employed as President at The Harper Agency. He studied Business Management at Belmont University. Find him on Facebook and Instagram.

    Ali’s mom Sharon works at Sharon Harper Designs. She went to Franklin County High School and then MTSU. On her birthday in 2019, Ali wished Sharon, “HBD to my favorite person in the world. Mom, I can’t imagine not talking to you 12 times a day and being with you all the time- you are not only beautiful, selfless, and caring; but you are my other half of my heart. I love you so much.”
